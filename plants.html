Plant Care

The Roots and Shoots home for plant care and advice.

Vegetables

Carrots

Carrots come in an array of varieties from orange to purple and white, can be easily stored, and are a versatile ingredient to enjoy.

Sowing: Feb-June

Harvest: May-Oct

Potatoes

It is also one of the easiest of things to grow and it beats anything you can buy from the supermarket on taste.

Sowing: Feb-Apr

Harvest: Aug-Sept

Green beans

Green beans are relatively easy to grow and can be either direct-sown outdoors or started indoors before planting out.

Sowing: Feb-Apr

Harvest: Aug-Sept

Lettuce

Lettuce is easy to grow, requires minimal space, and provides fresh, flavorful leaves for salads and other dishes.

Sowing: Feb-June

Harvest: June-July

Cucumber

Cucumbers are tender climbing or scrambling plants, for growing in a greenhouse or a warm sunny site outdoors, either up supports or trailing on the ground.

Sowing: Apr-May

Harvest: Aug-Sept

Cress

Garden cress is added to soups, sandwiches and salads for its tangy flavour. It is also eaten as sprouts, and the fresh or dried seed pods can be used as a peppery seasoning.

Sowing: Anytime

Harvest: 6 weeks after planting

Rhubarb

Rhubarb is easy to grow, producing masses of delicious stalks every year. Only the rhubarb stalks are edible. Never eat rhubarb leaves as these are extremely poisonous.

Sowing: Jan-Feb

Harvest: May-June

Fruit

Strawberries

Enjoy the taste of homegrown strawberries at their peak ripeness. Packed full of vitamin C and other health benifits.

Sowing: Jan-Feb

Harvest: June-Sept

Tomatoes

The main reason to grow your own tomatoes is that they taste so good, and they haven’t been sprayed with pesticides.

Sowing: Jan-Feb

Harvest: Aug-Sept

Lemon Tree

Growing your own lemons is a rewarding way to bring a little sunshine into your garden or home!

Sowing: Anytime

Harvest: 5+ years

Blackberries

Blackberries are hardy and productive—just give them a little attention, and you’ll enjoy baskets of juicy berries every summer!

Sowing: Sept-Oct

Harvest: June-Sept

House Plants

Orchid

Orchid care involves providing the right light and temperature, as well as using the appropriate potting mix and fertilizer. It is important not to overwater these plants.

Position: Indirect Sun

Watering: Weekly

Cacti

To successfully grow cacti, provide them with bright, indirect light, well-draining soil, and infrequent watering, especially during their dormancy period.

Position: Indirect Sun

Watering: Monthly

Hyacinth

Hyacinth care involves ensuring proper planting conditions, watering, and after-flowering care to promote healthy growth and future blooms. Hyacinths thrive in well-draining soil.

Position: Full, partial sun

Watering: Moist, well-drained
